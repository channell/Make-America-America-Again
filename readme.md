# Make America, America Again
![image](Make_America_America_Again.svg)

##Overview

**Make America, America Again** (`MAAA`) is a slogan and visual identity meant as a 
deliberate riposte to “Make America Great Again.” It does not argue that America cannot 
be great; it argues that America cannot be itself while unamerican ideas and unamerican people 
steer the country in an unamerican direction.

“America Again” is a call for national renewal: a return to constitutional self-government, 
civic duty, productive work, and public-spirited confidence. The tone is intentionally accusatory 
- because the claim is accusatory: that the country has been misled, and diverted away from 
an American path, and that it must be put back on it.

## Origin and reference

`MAAA` takes its cue from Langston Hughes’ poem Let America Be America Again - 
not as a quotation exercise, but as a framework: America is not an abstract brand, 
it is a set of obligations and aspirations that can be betrayed. Where “Great Again” 
implies a return to status, “America Again” implies a return to legitimacy.

## What the nostalgia is (and is not)

`MAAA` does not seek nostalgia for the 1950s. Its reference point is the Roosevelt era: 
a period of national mobilization and institutional seriousness, when America climbed 
out of depression, outproduced its enemies, and helped set a world order shaped by 
American industry, American technology, and American confidence. The message is simple: 
the country can do hard things again - once it stops indulging unamerican habits and 
unamerican leadership.

## Visual system

Geometry and tone. The iconography is deliberately simple, blocky, and geometric. 
It is meant to read as orderly and repeatable - an emblem you could stencil, stamp, or carve - 
suggesting harmony through structure rather than through ornament.
Color. The primary color is Old Glory Blue (the deep blue associated with the U.S. flag).
Use a single, flat blue wherever possible; avoid gradients and effects. 
A one-color black or white version is consistent when production requires it.

### Icon construction

The forms are drawn from the Broadway typeface associated with the Roosevelt years, 
using the geometry of its capital “A” as the seed shape. The intent is not typographic 
nostalgia for its own sake; it is to borrow a period-appropriate, public-works-era 
confidence and convert it into a modular symbol set.

### Pattern system

The `MAAA` icon is designed to tile. Repetition is not a decorative afterthought; 
it is part of the message - order, discipline, and cohesion instead of fragmentation.
